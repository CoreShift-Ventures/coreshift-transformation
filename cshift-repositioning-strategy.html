CoreShift Website Repositioning

From "Custom Business Systems" → "Intelligent Agents for Enterprise Operations." A section-by-section audit with exact rewrites.

Prepared for: Srinath Sridharan Date: February 2026 Scope: cshift.io full page rewrite

The Diagnosis

The site is beautifully designed — the orange/black palette, clean typography, and layout are professional and distinctive. The problem is entirely what it says, not how it looks.

Five Critical Problems

  1. Generic positioning. "Modern Business Systems for Scaling Companies" could be any of 10,000 dev shops in Bengaluru. There's nothing a CFO at Lucas TVS or a fund manager at ASK would recognize as "this is for me."
  2. Portfolio doesn't match reality. ClaimFlow, WorkMate, and SaaS Ops are marked "WIP" — they were never built. If a prospect clicks "View all 3 screens" and then asks for a reference, you have a credibility problem.
  3. Selling code, not outcomes. The site talks about React, PostgreSQL, Tailwind, Python. Your buyers (CFOs, COOs, fund managers) don't care about your stack. They care about their invoices reconciling and reports generating.
  4. No recurring revenue story. The "Diagnose → Build → Scale" model reads as one-time project work. There's no "we stay with you" proposition. The agent-as-a-service model doesn't exist on the site.
  5. Competing in a dying market. "We build AI-powered systems around your process" is now table stakes. A CA with Claude Code can build a basic web app in a weekend. The moat has to be elsewhere — and it is, but the site doesn't communicate it.

Competitor Section Rewrite

Update Needed

The current "Consultancies vs Generic SaaS vs Dev Agencies" structure is solid. But in 2026, the biggest competitor isn't dev agencies — it's DIY AI tools. The section needs a fourth column or a complete reframing.

New Headline: "Why Not Just Use AI Yourself?"

Option A: "Build It Yourself with AI"

Anyone can generate code with ChatGPT now. But who configures the SAP OData connection? Who handles the vendor portal that changes its HTML monthly? Who troubleshoots at 3am when the batch fails?

→ AI writes code. It doesn't run operations.

Option B: "Buy a Generic SaaS / RPA Tool"

Off-the-shelf automation tools don't understand your fund's waterfall structure, your specific vendor portal layouts, or your SAP customizations. You'll spend 6 months configuring what we deploy in 2 weeks.

→ Generic tools require you to fit their box. Our agents are built around your process.

Option C: "Hire a Dev Shop"

Dev shops build what you ask for and walk away. When the vendor portal changes, when SAP gets upgraded, when a new compliance requirement hits — they're gone. We stay. Because we operate the agents, not just build them.

→ You don't need a builder. You need an operations partner.

New Process Detail

Step 1: Discover (Week 1)

We map your operational workflow — every portal login, every Excel copy-paste, every manual step. We identify what should be automated and design the agent specification.

→ Outcome: A clear automation blueprint with expected savings and ROI projection.

Step 2: Deploy (Week 2-3)

We build, configure, and deploy your agent — extraction scripts, validation logic, SAP/Tally integration. Deployed on your infrastructure. Runs in parallel with your team for validation.

→ Outcome: Agent running in production, processing real data on your machines.

Step 3: Operate (Ongoing)

We monitor, maintain, and improve your agents. When vendor portals change, when new requirements emerge, when edge cases appear — we handle it. You just see the results in your monthly performance report.

→ Outcome: Operations that run without your team lifting a finger. ₹50-75K/month.
